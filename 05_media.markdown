This page collates **selected** press items that I directly contributed to, such as interviews. It doesn't include other media coverage of my work.

[Software met open broncode maakt ons onafhankelijker](https://www.nemokennislink.nl/publicaties/software-met-open-broncode-maakt-ons-onafhankelijker/). Contribution to a collective interview for NEMO Kennislink (in Dutch) on the potential for open-source software.

[RVO funding to transform Dutch greenhouses into innovative energy hubs](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/2026/tbm/rvo-funding-to-transform-dutch-greenhouses-into-innovative-energy-hubs). Interview by the TU Delft on the goals and expected societal impact of the [SPROUT project](/projects).

[Boosting the energy transition by looking further than the cost-optimal solution](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/2025/tbm/boosting-the-energy-transition-by-looking-further-than-the-cost-optimal-solution). Interview by the TU Delft on the opinion article advocating for [MGA](/research-outputs) to become the new standard in energy system planning.

[What would people do? Putting stakeholder preferences back in Energy System Modeling ](https://www.tudelft.nl/powerweb/news-and-activities/blogs/blog-1). Interview by the TU Delft PowerWeb institute on the development of the human-in-the-loop version of [MGA methods to design energy systems](/research-outputs).

[Behind the paper: ‘human-in-the-loop’ energy system design](https://latitude.plos.org/2025/03/behind-the-paper-human-in-the-loop-energy-system-design/). Interview by PLOS's blog Latitude, also focused on the human-in-the-loop approach above.

[Wat voor energie wil jij in 2050? Draai zelf aan de knoppen van een Europees energiemodel](https://www.nemokennislink.nl/publicaties/wat-voor-energie-wil-jij-in-2050/). Interview for NEMO Kennislink (in Dutch) on the societal implications of a publication using [MGA](/research-outputs) to reveal many options to make Europe carbon-neutral and energy-independent.

[TU Delft researchers develop model for green, energy independent Europe](https://www.tudelft.nl/en/2022/tu-delft/tu-delft-researchers-develop-model-for-green-energy-independent-europe). Interview by the TU Delft on the article covered by NEMO Kennislink above, investigating options for a carbon-neutral and energy-independent Europe.
